# Product Marketing Context

*Last updated: April 10, 2026*

## Product Overview
**One-liner:** Daily Sparks helps IB families build calmer daily reading habits for MYP and DP through direct student delivery, structured reflection, and parent-visible follow-through.

**What it does:** Daily Sparks turns one daily reading brief into a repeatable home learning loop. Students receive a structured brief, families keep a searchable archive, and the workflow extends into notebook capture and weekly recap instead of ending at the first read.

**Product category:** IB reading support, IB family study workflow, MYP reading support, DP reading and writing support.

**Product type:** Subscription software for families.

**Business model:** Self-serve monthly and yearly subscription with a 7-day free trial.

## Target Audience
**Target companies:** N/A. Primary audience is parent buyers in IB households.

**Decision-makers:** Parents and guardians supporting IB students.

**Primary use case:** Create a calmer, consistent daily reading habit that supports academic growth without adding another chaotic app.

**Jobs to be done:**
- Help my child keep a daily reading routine that feels manageable.
- Turn reading into something reusable for inquiry, writing, and reflection.
- Let me stay informed without micromanaging every student session.

**Use cases:**
- MYP families who want bridge reading, global context, and inquiry prompts.
- DP families who want academic framing, claim building, evidence limits, and TOK-style thinking.
- Parents who want Goodnotes delivery for the student and Notion archive for the household.

## Personas
| Persona | Cares about | Challenge | Value we promise |
|---------|-------------|-----------|------------------|
| IB parent buyer | consistency, visible progress, low drama | child reads inconsistently and tools feel fragmented | a calmer routine with clear parent visibility |
| MYP learner | manageable reading, relevance, context | current affairs feels abstract or too difficult | bridge reading with inquiry and compare-connect moves |
| DP learner | writing quality, usable ideas, structure | reads articles but does not convert them into arguments | daily briefs that surface claim, counterpoint, and TOK prompts |

## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem:** Families want strong reading habits, but most current workflows are either too noisy, too generic, or too hard to sustain daily.

**Why alternatives fall short:**
- news apps create distraction, not academic follow-through
- generic reading platforms do not differentiate MYP and DP needs
- parent oversight often lives in scattered files, screenshots, and old emails

**What it costs them:** inconsistent reading, weak reflection habits, lower confidence in writing and argument quality, and more family friction around school support.

**Emotional tension:** guilt, uncertainty, fear of falling behind, and frustration with fragmented tools.

## Competitive Landscape
**Direct:** reading-summary tools and IB support products — often too generic, too exam-only, or too noisy.

**Secondary:** newsletters, article apps, and Google Classroom style flows — they deliver content but not a full family learning loop.

**Indirect:** parents manually curating articles and notes — flexible, but not scalable or consistent.

## Differentiation
**Key differentiators:**
- one source, programme-native MYP / DP reading outputs
- Goodnotes for student delivery
- Notion for family archive
- notebook capture and weekly recap loops

**How we do it differently:** we treat reading as a daily operating system for families, not a one-off article feed.

**Why that's better:** the workflow survives beyond the first click and becomes visible, searchable, and reusable.

**Why customers choose us:** calmer routine, lower friction, clearer parent visibility, and stronger stage-specific academic framing.

## Objections
| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| My child already reads articles online. | Daily Sparks is not just another article source; it structures the thinking and follow-through around each brief. |
| We do not want another dashboard. | The workflow is intentionally split: student delivery in Goodnotes, parent visibility in Notion, and a lighter dashboard. |
| I am not sure whether my child is MYP or DP ready for this. | The product is explicitly split into MYP bridge reading and DP academic framing, with public guides and starter-kit onboarding. |

**Anti-persona:** families looking for pure entertainment news content, full school LMS replacement, or purely teacher-facing classroom software.

## Switching Dynamics
**Push:** distraction, inconsistency, and lack of academic carryover from ordinary reading.

**Pull:** calmer workflow, stage-specific structure, and visible archive / recap loops.

**Habit:** existing student habits around random article browsing or scattered notes.

**Anxiety:** setup friction, uncertainty about Goodnotes / Notion, and doubt about daily consistency.
