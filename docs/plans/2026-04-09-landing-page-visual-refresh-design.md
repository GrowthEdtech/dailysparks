# Landing Page Visual Refresh Design

## Goal

Replace the weak placeholder-style illustration in the homepage `How it works`
section with a visual that actually explains the product loop.

The image should feel consistent with the current landing page, but it should
communicate:

- a daily brief arriving in a calm reading surface
- `MYP` inquiry and context signals
- `DP` claim and argument signals
- Goodnotes delivery
- Notion archive and recap reuse

## Problem

The current illustration is mostly a dark panel with a laptop emoji and a short
line of text. It reads like a filler panel instead of a product story.

That creates two gaps:

1. it does not reinforce the `Read, reflect, and save` step
2. it misses the chance to visually connect `MYP / DP / Goodnotes / Notion`

## Product Direction

Keep the current outer framing:

- light rounded outer shell
- dark inner stage
- small `IB MYP · DP` decal

Replace only the interior artwork with a `student reading workspace` scene.

## Recommended Composition

### Center

A larger `Daily brief` card should sit in the middle as the main focus.

It should imply a real structured packet rather than a generic device graphic.

### Surrounding signals

Around the main brief, use smaller floating cards or chips for:

- `MYP inquiry lens`
- `DP claim builder`
- `Goodnotes delivery`
- `Notion archive`
- `Weekly recap`

These should feel like one workflow rather than five isolated badges.

### Motion and mood

Use subtle gradients, soft shadows, and faint connector lines.

The panel should feel:

- calm
- academic
- structured
- modern

It should not feel cartoonish or emoji-based.

## Copy Direction

The illustration should avoid generic copy like `Wait until you see their first analysis.`

Use short product-native labels instead:

- `Daily brief`
- `MYP inquiry lens`
- `DP claim builder`
- `Goodnotes delivery`
- `Notion archive`
- `Weekly recap`

## Non-goals

This slice does not include:

- changing the layout of the `How it works` section
- changing the decal
- adding a real screenshot
- changing CTA copy
- changing pricing or integrations

## Success Criteria

- the panel feels intentionally designed, not like a placeholder
- the visual reinforces the `Read, reflect, and save` workflow
- `MYP` and `DP` are both present without splitting the composition
- the artwork remains lightweight and fully code-native
